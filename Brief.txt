Assignment Overview
This assignment requires you to engage with generative artificial intelligence from a technical, analytical, and systems-oriented perspective, while remaining grounded in media applications. You will design and realise an original media artefact, system, or advanced project prototype that integrates generative AI in a substantive and methodologically rigorous way, supported by a critical technical and contextual analysis.
The emphasis is placed on technical understanding, system design, experimental evaluation, and your ability to situate media-focused AI work within current research, industry practice, and ethical debates surrounding generative AI.
Your work must:
•	Demonstrate technical understanding of the generative AI methods used
•	Show evidence of system design, experimentation, and iteration
•	Go beyond surface-level or off-the-shelf usage through configuration, integration, or customisation
•	Be underpinned by a clear technical and conceptual rationale
Submission method: Brightspace assignment (Large/multiple file) with code on GitHub
Can I use Generative AI tools?
You are expected to engage critically with generative AI tools as part of this assignment. You must:
•	Fully document and disclose all AI tools, models, and systems used
•	Reflect critically on tool limitations, training assumptions, and biases
•	Retain responsibility for all creative and analytical decisions
Undeclared or uncritical use of AI may be considered academic misconduct.
Assessment Criteria
Your submission will be assessed on:
•	Conceptual depth and originality
•	Critical engagement with generative AI technologies
•	Integration of theory, research, and creative practice
•	Ethical, legal, and professional awareness
•	Quality, clarity, and academic rigour of written analysis

Assignment Task
You are required to:
1.	Design and produce an original media artefact or advanced project prototype that makes substantive use of generative AI, and
2.	Submit a critical reflective paper that contextualises, documents, and evaluates your practice.
Media Artefact / Project Output
Your project output may take one of the following forms:
•	A functional media-focused AI system, tool, or pipeline (e.g. generative video/audio system, multimodal application, creative coding project, interactive AI media platform)
•	A completed media artefact generated through a custom or non-trivial AI workflow such as multimedia news package (text, image, and short-form video) about a futuristic or speculative technological development.
•	A research-informed prototype or proof of concept (including model selection, system architecture, prompt or parameter strategies, and evaluation approach)
Critical Reflective Paper
Your written submission should be structured as a technical-critical report and address the following areas:
•	Problem definition and context: Media challenge or opportunity being addressed, situated within relevant research or industry practice
•	System design and methodology: Model choices, architectures, data sources, prompt or parameter strategies, and implementation details
•	Experimental process and evaluation: How outputs or system behaviour were tested, compared, or assessed
•	Human–AI interaction: Division of labour, control mechanisms, and user or creator agency
•	Critical analysis: Technical strengths, limitations, failure modes, and trade-offs
•	Ethical, legal, and societal considerations: Bias, copyright, data provenance, transparency, sustainability, and deployment risks
•	Reflection and future work: Potential improvements, extensions, or research directions
Submission Requirements:
•	Media artefact or project materials
•	Critical reflective paper (PDF)
•	Supporting documentation (e.g. prompt logs, system diagrams, workflow documentation)
Submission method: 
Brightspace assignment (Turnitin) / Brightspace assignment (Large/multiple file)
